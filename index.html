Mean And Medium

6.29.2015 - Lynda JS Code Clinic AJAX JSON

In the Lynda challenge, the goal was to calculate the mean and median of the wind speed, air temperature, and barometric pressure, recorded at the Deep Moor station for a given range of dates using javascript as the chosen language. The data would be taken from a list the US Navy compiles during their submarine testing in Lake Pend Oreille. Weather statistics such as wind speed, air temperature, and barometric pressure should be ajaxed in by using a restful api that returned a jsonp object filled with data from this site: http://lpo.dt.navy.mil.

Reflections

For this exercise I was just concentrating on getting a successful response from the api and writing a mean and median functionality. After comparing my solutions to the offered solutions, there were a few things that really stood out to me that I hope to take away. Firstly, the lynda solutions did a much better job of abstracting out the functionality and keeping code simple. For example, their solutions to the mean function simply takes an array argument and uses the reduce method to add everything up in that array argument, and simply divides that sum by the total number of the items in the array. The final step was setting the returned mean to a nicer fixed point value.
I'm happy that my mean solution returned the correct mean values, but becuase I didn't know about the reduce method I feel that my function was a bit more cumbersome. Instead, I used a for loop to loop through the length of the array, and used the counter to slice each individual item into a variable to be parsed to a floating point integer. Then, I used an additional assignment operator to add it to a 'MeanTotal' variable. Once the loop was finished, the total sum of the array values were divided by the total array length giving the accurate mean value (possibly too accurate, I probably should've used the toFixed function to return a nice number).

Going Further

The lesson went on to actually complete the solution really thouroughly. They visualized the data and created drop down menus to dynamically update the range of dates. While those weren't in the scope of my personal goal for the challenge, I followed along and completed the interesting parts of the challenge. It was really the first time I had seen and used the c3 library, which is an impressive tool. Also, it was really good to go through the neccessary steps to massage the data so c3 could display the data correctly.
